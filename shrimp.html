Shrimp & Corn Chowder

Creamy, sweet, and savory—all in one bowl.

Servings: 4
Prep Time: 15 minutes
Cook Time: 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons butter
  • 1 medium yellow onion, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • ⅓ cup all-purpose flour
  • 4 cups seafood or vegetable broth
  • 2 cups heavy cream (or half-and-half)
  • 2 cups corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1 lb (450 g) shrimp, peeled & deveined
  • 1 teaspoon Old Bay seasoning (or seafood seasoning)
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • Salt & freshly ground pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley (for garnish)
  • Lemon wedges (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Sauté the vegetables: In a large pot over medium heat, melt the butter. Add the onion, celery, carrots, and red pepper; cook, stirring occasionally, until the onion is translucent and the veggies begin to soften, about 5–6 minutes. Stir in the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds.
  2. Make the roux: Sprinkle the flour over the veggies and stir continuously for 1–2 minutes, until the flour is lightly golden and coats the vegetables.
  3. Add liquids & corn: Slowly whisk in the broth, making sure there are no lumps. Add the corn, Old Bay seasoning, and smoked paprika. Bring to a gentle simmer and cook, uncovered, for 10 minutes—this thickens the base and cooks the corn.
  4. Cook the shrimp: Season the shrimp lightly with salt and pepper and drop them into the simmering chowder. Cook until the shrimp turn pink and opaque, about 3–4 minutes.
  5. Finish with cream: Reduce heat to low. Stir in the heavy cream and warm through for 2 more minutes—do not boil once the cream is added. Taste and adjust seasoning with salt, pepper, or a squeeze of lemon.
  6. Serve: Ladle into bowls, sprinkle with chopped parsley, and pass lemon wedges at the table for a bright finish.

Tips & Variations

  • Smoky bacon boost: Start by rendering 4 slices chopped bacon in the pot, remove it before adding butter, then sprinkle crispy bits over each bowl.
  • Thicker chowder: Swap half the broth for milk and simmer an extra 5 minutes before adding cream.
  • Shellfish swap: Replace shrimp with bite-sized scallops or a medley of clams and mussels (add clams/mussels at step 3 and cook until they open).
  • Spice it up: A pinch of cayenne or a dash of hot sauce adds a nice kick.
  • Herb notes: Fresh thyme or chives make a lovely garnish in place of parsley.
