Classic Ukrainian Borscht

Rich beet soup with beef, served with sour cream.

Servings: 6–8
Prep Time: 20 minutes
Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons sunflower oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 1 beet, peeled and grated
  • ½ small head of green cabbage, shredded
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 6 cups beef broth
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 200–300g beef (with bone, like short ribs or shank)
  • Salt & pepper, to taste
  • Fresh dill, chopped (for garnish)
  • Sour cream (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Cook the beef: Place beef in a large pot and cover with water. Bring to a boil, skimming off any foam. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for about 45 minutes to 1 hour until tender. Remove beef, cut into bite-sized pieces, and return to the pot.
  2. Sauté the vegetables: In a separate pan, heat sunflower oil over medium heat. Add onion and carrots; cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Add beets and tomato paste; cook another 5–7 minutes until vibrant and tender.
  3. Combine and simmer: Add sautéed vegetables to the pot with beef broth. Stir in potatoes, cabbage, and bay leaf. Simmer uncovered for 20–25 minutes until vegetables are soft.
  4. Season and rest: Season with salt and pepper to taste. Turn off the heat and let the borscht sit covered for 10–15 minutes to deepen the flavor.
  5. Serve warm: Ladle borscht into bowls. Top with a dollop of sour cream and sprinkle with fresh dill. Serve with rye bread or garlic pampushky if desired.
